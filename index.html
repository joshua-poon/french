Why learn French?

French is a good language to learn as a second language. Here are some syntactical reasons why:

  • It possesses many properties of foreign languages: conjugations, genders, stricter word order. You understand the different tenses and how they can be used.
  • You understand the fact that there is no such thing as 'word to word' translation, but sometimes, whole meanings needs to be translated together.
  • Differing word order such as noun-adjective, which are also transferable to other languages including English compound nouns of French origin
  • Analysing the syntactic structure of a sentence becomes second nature, as it is one ofthe methods of thorough translation. Understanding concepts such as singular vs polite forms of a pronoun.

Also, the vocabulary is useful in a number of ways.

  • Firstly, being able to see connections with the English language, its similarities, which is a method of memorising vocabulary which I will delve into.
  • how it has influenced the etymology of many English words, especially concepts such as words with different levels of prestige based on their Romantic roots

Pronunciation of French words is a whole nother game. I'll also be looking into this, but in terms of the evolution of French pronunciation, I can't say that I am that knowledgeable in that area.
